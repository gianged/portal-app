================================================================
INTERNAL PORTAL - BUSINESS LOGIC
================================================================

This document describes the domain rules of the portal: who the
actors are, what entities exist, how those entities move through
their lifecycles, and what each actor is allowed to do. It does
not cover infrastructure or implementation details.


----------------------------------------------------------------
ACTORS
----------------------------------------------------------------

The portal has six distinct actor types. Roles are not mutually
exclusive: a single user can hold multiple roles. For example, a
Group Leader is also a Member of their group, and a Director can
simultaneously be a Group Leader of one team.

Director
    Has read access to everything in the company. Sees all
    projects, all tickets, all group chats (with the deliberate
    exception of direct messages between individuals). Directors
    can intervene in any workflow but typically do not own
    day-to-day work.

HR
    Manages staff records. Creates new users, assigns them to
    groups, sets their roles within those groups, and deactivates
    them when they leave. HR is also the only role permitted to
    post company-wide announcements.

Group Leader
    Manages a single group's work. Owns the group's projects,
    invites other groups to collaborate, assigns requests within
    those projects, and posts announcements in their group's
    chat. Each group has exactly one leader at any time.

Group Sub-Leader
    Assists the leader. Can view and assign requests, post
    announcements in the group chat, but cannot transfer
    ownership of projects or invite other groups. A group can
    have multiple sub-leaders.

Member (Staff)
    Regular employee. Belongs to one or more groups, contributes
    to projects, chats with colleagues, raises IT tickets when
    something breaks. The largest population by count.

IT
    Special-purpose group that handles support tickets. Members
    of the IT group can triage, assign, and resolve tickets
    raised by anyone in the company. Otherwise behaves like any
    other group.


----------------------------------------------------------------
CORE ENTITIES
----------------------------------------------------------------

User
    A person with an account. Has a profile (name, avatar,
    contact info, timezone), belongs to one or more groups, and
    holds zero or more roles per group. A user has exactly one
    lifecycle state at any time: pending, active, or deactivated.

Group
    An organizational unit. Has a name, a description, a single
    leader, zero or more sub-leaders, and zero or more members.
    A user's relationship to a group is one of: leader,
    sub-leader, or member. Groups cannot be nested; the org
    structure is flat at the group level. Hierarchy lives in
    user role, not group containment.

Project
    A body of work owned by a group. Has a name, description,
    status, owner group, and zero or more collaborator groups.
    Contains many Requests. Each project has exactly one owner
    group at any time. Ownership can be transferred between
    groups but only by mutual consent of both leaders.

Request
    A unit of work within a project. Has a title, description,
    creator, optional assignee, status, priority, optional due
    date, and optional attachments. Belongs to exactly one
    project.

Ticket
    A support request from any employee to the IT group.
    Structurally similar to a Request but lives outside the
    project hierarchy. Has a requester, optional assignee
    (an IT member), status, priority, and category (hardware,
    software, access, other).

Channel
    A container for chat messages. Three kinds:
      - Group channel: one per group, members of the group
        participate.
      - General channel: one for the whole company, every active
        user participates.
      - Direct channel: one per pair of users, only the two
        participants see it.

Message
    A chat message within a channel. Has a sender, body,
    timestamp, optional attachments, optional mentions, and an
    is_announcement flag.

Announcement
    A message with the is_announcement flag set. Cannot be
    edited after a grace period (15 minutes), cannot be deleted
    by anyone except its sender or HR.


----------------------------------------------------------------
USER LIFECYCLE
----------------------------------------------------------------

States:

    pending      Account created by HR, user has not yet
                 completed first login.
    active       Normal operating state.
    deactivated  Soft-deleted. Cannot log in, filtered from
                 active queries, but historical references
                 (authored messages, created requests, etc.)
                 remain intact.

Transitions:

    (none) ─create by HR──> pending
    pending ─first login──> active
    active ─deactivate by HR──> deactivated
    deactivated ─reactivate by HR──> active

Rules:

    A pending user has no access to anything until they complete
    first login.

    Deactivation does not remove the user from their groups
    immediately. Instead, the user's group memberships are
    marked inactive. This preserves the structure for audit
    while removing access.

    Before deactivating a user, HR must resolve any active
    assignments: open requests assigned to the user must be
    reassigned or cancelled, and group leadership must be
    transferred to another member.

    A deactivated user's chat messages, request authorship, and
    project contributions remain visible in the system. Their
    name is rendered as their original name with a "(former
    employee)" suffix in the UI.


----------------------------------------------------------------
GROUP MEMBERSHIP RULES
----------------------------------------------------------------

A group has exactly one leader. This is enforced at the database
level via a partial unique constraint.

A group can have any number of sub-leaders, including zero.

A user can be a member of multiple groups. Their role can differ
per group: leader of group A, member of group B, sub-leader of
group C.

When a group leader is deactivated, HR must transfer leadership
to another member (typically a sub-leader, but not required) as
part of the deactivation flow. A group cannot exist without a
leader.

A group cannot be deleted while it has active projects. The
projects must be transferred to another owner group or completed
first.


----------------------------------------------------------------
PROJECT LIFECYCLE
----------------------------------------------------------------

States:

    planning    Initial state. Project exists but work has not
                started. Visible only to the owner group.
    active      Work in progress. Visible to owner and
                collaborator groups.
    on_hold    Temporarily paused. Read-only for everyone
                except the owner group leader.
    completed   Work finished. Read-only for all.
    cancelled   Project abandoned. Read-only for all.

Transitions:

    (none) ─create by group leader──> planning
    planning ─activate──> active
    active ─pause──> on_hold
    on_hold ─resume──> active
    active ─complete──> completed
    active ─cancel──> cancelled
    planning ─cancel──> cancelled

Rules:

    Only group leaders can create projects, and the project is
    automatically owned by the leader's group.

    Project status transitions can only be initiated by the
    owner group leader.

    A project in planning state has no collaborator groups
    visible to it; collaborators can only be invited after the
    project enters active state.

    Completing or cancelling a project automatically closes all
    its open requests (status moves to cancelled with a note).


----------------------------------------------------------------
PROJECT COLLABORATION (GROUP INVITES)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Project invites are group-level. When a project owner wants to
bring another group into a project, they invite the group as a
whole. All members of the invited group gain access; subsequent
membership changes propagate automatically.

Invite states:

    pending     Invite sent, target group leader has not
                responded.
    accepted    Invited group is now a collaborator.
    declined    Invited group declined.
    revoked     Owner group leader withdrew the invite before
                it was accepted, or removed the collaborator
                group later.

Transitions:

    owner sends invite        ─> pending
    target leader accepts     ─> accepted
    target leader declines    ─> declined
    owner revokes (pre-accept)─> revoked
    owner removes (post-accept)─> revoked

Rules:

    Only the owner group's leader can send or revoke invites.

    Only the target group's leader can accept or decline an
    incoming invite.

    A group cannot be invited twice to the same project; if an
    invite was declined or revoked, the owner can send a new
    one, replacing the old record.

    Owner group cannot be a collaborator of its own project.

    When a collaborator group's membership changes, project
    access automatically reflects the change. No project-level
    bookkeeping is needed.


----------------------------------------------------------------
REQUEST LIFECYCLE
----------------------------------------------------------------

States:

    draft       Creator is still composing. Visible only to
                creator.
    submitted   Ready for triage. No assignee yet.
    assigned    Has an assignee, work has not started.
    in_progress Assignee is working on it.
    review      Assignee marked done, awaiting verification by
                creator or project leader.
    completed   Verified done.
    cancelled   Abandoned at any prior state.

Transitions:

    (none) ─create──> draft
    draft ─submit──> submitted
    submitted ─assign──> assigned
    assigned ─start──> in_progress
    in_progress ─finish──> review
    review ─approve──> completed
    review ─reject──> in_progress
    {any except completed} ─cancel──> cancelled

Rules:

    Anyone with access to the project (owner group members or
    collaborator group members) can create a request.

    Only the owner group's leader or sub-leaders can assign a
    request to a specific user. Assignees must be members of
    the owner group or a collaborator group.

    Only the assignee can move a request from assigned to
    in_progress, and from in_progress to review.

    Only the creator, the project owner leader, or a
    sub-leader can approve a review or reject it back to
    in_progress.

    Once completed, a request cannot be reopened. A new request
    must be created if follow-up work is needed.


----------------------------------------------------------------
TICKET LIFECYCLE
----------------------------------------------------------------

States:

    open        Just raised, no IT staff has looked at it yet.
    triaged     An IT member has reviewed it and set priority.
    assigned    Specific IT member is responsible for it.
    in_progress IT member is working on it.
    resolved    IT believes the issue is fixed, awaiting
                requester confirmation.
    closed      Requester confirmed, ticket archived.
    reopened    Requester rejected resolution, returns to
                in_progress.

Transitions:

    (none) ─raise──> open
    open ─triage──> triaged
    triaged ─assign──> assigned
    assigned ─start──> in_progress
    in_progress ─resolve──> resolved
    resolved ─confirm──> closed
    resolved ─reject──> in_progress (reopened)
    closed ─reopen within 7 days──> in_progress (reopened)

Rules:

    Any active user can raise a ticket.

    Only IT group members can move tickets through the triage,
    assigned, in_progress, and resolved states.

    Only the requester can move a ticket from resolved to closed
    or reject it back to in_progress.

    After a ticket has been closed for 7 days, it cannot be
    reopened. A new ticket must be raised.

    Tickets carry a priority (low, normal, high, urgent) set
    during triage. Urgent tickets generate a notification to all
    IT members immediately.


----------------------------------------------------------------
CHAT RULES
----------------------------------------------------------------

Group channels

    One channel per group. Members of the group automatically
    have read/write access. Non-members cannot see the channel
    in their channel list.

    Group leader and sub-leaders can post announcements
    (is_announcement flag) within the group channel.

    Group leader can delete any message in their group's
    channel. Members can delete only their own messages within
    a 15-minute grace period.

General channel

    One channel for the entire company. Every active user has
    read access. Write access is restricted to HR only.

    The general channel is announcement-only in effect: HR
    posts, everyone reads. This is intentional, to prevent the
    general channel from becoming noise.

Direct channels

    Created on demand between any two active users. Visible
    only to the two participants, including to Directors.
    Directors do not have a backdoor into direct messages.

    No announcements in direct channels.

    Either participant can delete their own messages within a
    15-minute grace period. Neither can delete the other's.


----------------------------------------------------------------
ANNOUNCEMENTS
----------------------------------------------------------------

An announcement is a message with the is_announcement flag set.
It is rendered differently in the UI (pinned, highlighted, or
similar) and triggers a notification to all channel members.

Posting rules:

    Group channel announcements: posted by the group's leader or
    sub-leaders.

    General channel announcements: posted by HR only.

    Direct channel: announcements not permitted.

Editing:

    Announcements can be edited only within 15 minutes of
    posting. After that, they are immutable. A correction must
    be posted as a new announcement.

    Edits during the grace period are logged; the announcement
    shows an "edited" marker.

Deletion:

    Only the sender or HR can delete an announcement, at any
    time. Deletion is hard delete for announcements (unlike
    regular messages, which are soft-deleted) because they
    were intentionally broadcast.


----------------------------------------------------------------
PERMISSIONS SUMMARY
----------------------------------------------------------------

The matrix below summarizes who can perform each major action.
"Owner leader" means the leader of the project's owner group.
"Member" means a member of the owner or any collaborator group
of the relevant project.

Action                          Allowed actors
------                          --------------
Create user                     HR
Deactivate user                 HR
Assign user to group            HR
Change user's role in group     HR
Create group                    HR
Delete group                    HR (with no active projects)
Create project                  Group leader (in their group)
Edit project metadata           Owner leader, sub-leaders
Change project status           Owner leader
Invite group to project         Owner leader
Accept project invite           Target group leader
Remove collaborator group       Owner leader
Create request                  Member
Assign request                  Owner leader, sub-leaders
Update request status           Assignee, owner leader,
                                sub-leaders (per state rules)
Raise ticket                    Any active user
Triage / assign ticket          IT member
Close ticket                    Requester
Send group chat message         Group member
Send direct message             Either participant
Send general chat message       HR
Post group announcement         Group leader, sub-leaders
Post general announcement       HR
Read everything (within scope)  Director


----------------------------------------------------------------
CROSS-CUTTING INVARIANTS
----------------------------------------------------------------

These hold across the entire system, regardless of state.

    1. Every group has exactly one leader at any time.

    2. Every project has exactly one owner group at any time.

    3. A user belongs to a group via exactly one role (leader,
       sub-leader, or member). No user has multiple roles in
       the same group.

    4. Deactivated users do not appear in active queries but
       remain referenced in historical records.

    5. Audit log entries are immutable. They reference user IDs
       even after deactivation.

    6. Announcements cannot be silently mutated after the
       15-minute grace period.

    7. A project's owner group cannot also be one of its
       collaborator groups.

    8. Direct message channels involve exactly two distinct
       users.

    9. The IT group is structurally a normal group, but its
       members have additional capabilities on tickets.

    10. Directors have read access to all non-direct
        communication. Direct messages are private even from
        Directors.


----------------------------------------------------------------
NOTES AND OPEN QUESTIONS
----------------------------------------------------------------

A few design decisions that aren't fully settled. Worth deciding
explicitly before implementation, even if the answer is "do the
simplest thing for now":

    Should non-leaders in a group be able to create requests in
    the group's projects, or only contribute to existing ones?
    The current rules say members can create requests; tighten
    if needed.

    Should Directors be visible to Group Leaders when viewing
    their group's project membership? Probably yes; Directors
    are implicitly attached to every group's view.

    Should there be a "watcher" or "follower" concept on
    requests and tickets, where uninvolved users can subscribe
    to status changes? Useful but adds notification complexity.

    Should announcements support scheduled posting (write now,
    publish at 9am tomorrow)? Out of scope initially.

    Should the IT group be a single group, or should there be
    sub-categories of IT support (network, software, etc.)?
    Starting with a single group is simpler.
